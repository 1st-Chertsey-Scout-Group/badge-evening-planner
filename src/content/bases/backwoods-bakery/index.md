The bakery only opens once the patrol's own fire is ready. First job is
naming a fire boss: they own the marked circle, the bucket and the final say
until the embers are dead.

- Fire first: mark the circle, fill the bucket, clear the ground to bare
  earth, then build a small lay in the pit and light it. Let it burn down -
  flames burn bread, embers bake it.
- Dough while it settles: flour, a pinch of salt and a splash of water in a
  zip-lock bag - clean hands first - squeezed to a stiff dough. Spiral a thin
  sausage round the foiled end of a stick and pinch it on. Thin twists cook;
  fat lumps char outside and stay raw inside.
- Bake low over the embers at the circle's edge, turning slowly. Done when
  it's golden, sounds hollow when tapped and slides off clean.
- Service: butter and jam into the hollow middle and a finished twist served
  to the leader. A star each for golden crust, cooked centre and a clean
  serve; the patrol eats the rest.

Dietary needs checked before the flour comes out; the fire boss holds the
circle, and the base ends with drown, stir, drown until the pit is hand-cold.
