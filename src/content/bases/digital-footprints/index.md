---
title: Digital footprints
description: Peg a whole day of clicks and posts on a washing line, then turn detective on a volunteer leader and write the plan that shrinks a footprint.
duration: 15
equipment:
  - Ball of string and clothes pegs for the washing line
  - Paper footprints in two colours
  - Green and red sticker dots
  - Marker pens
  - Laptop with wifi
  - A leader briefed and willing to be searched live

String a washing line across the corner and hand out footprints: this is
yesterday, online, pegged out in public.

- Peg your digital day, one footprint per action - every search, message,
  post, tag and video watched. Then walk the line and sticker each one:
  green if you are happy that trace exists somewhere, red if it is leakier
  than you thought. Argue the reds out as a patrol - who can actually see
  that, and is that OK?
- Second colour of footprint: what other people put up about you - tagged
  photos, the team sheet on the club site, a parent's birthday post. Peg
  them on. Some of it is useful, some of it nobody asked you about. Patrol
  settles its policy: ask before posting, untag, report.
- Detective round: the volunteer leader goes under the microscope. Patrol
  calls the search terms, laptop on the table, and tallies the haul - what
  came up, what surprised them, what would they want gone?
- Quick self-search on phones (results stay private, skip anything
  uncomfortable), then each scout writes the leader's - and their own -
  three-move lockdown: accounts to private, untag and delete the worst,
  ask the poster or the site to take things down, and search yourself
  every term-time to check it worked.

Best lockdown plan wins; the leader has to actually do it by next week.
