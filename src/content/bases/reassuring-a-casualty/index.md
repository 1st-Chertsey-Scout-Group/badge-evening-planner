Casualty drama school. The casualties are the actors: each draws an emotion
card and plays it hard - panicking, embarrassed, angry, or worryingly quiet.

- A leader opens as the world's worst first aider: looming over the casualty,
  shouting "DOES IT HURT?", waving the crowd in for a look. Let the patrol
  tear the performance apart, then build the right way from the wreckage.
- First aiders practise the real moves: say your name, ask theirs and use it,
  get down to their level, keep your voice low and slow.
- Tell them what's happening - "help is coming, I'm staying with you" - and
  keep talking, even when there's nothing new to say.
- Crowd control is care too: one scout plays the nosy bystander to move back
  politely but firmly.

Rotate so everyone reassures each emotion at least once. The skill being
drilled: you are treating fear, and fear responds to calm.
