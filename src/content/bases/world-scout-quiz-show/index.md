---
title: World scout quiz show
description: A buzzer quiz on 57 million scouts worldwide, then learn to introduce yourself in a language nobody at the table speaks.
duration: 15
equipment:
  - Quiz cards with answers on the back
  - Two squeaky toys as buzzers
  - World map to point at between rounds
  - Phrase cards with phonetic spellings (Swahili, Japanese and Polish sets)
  - Scoreboard, marker and a cardboard world cup for the winners
tags:
  - Scouts
  - Global
  - Indoor
requirements:
  - '40990'
  - '40995'
---

Gameshow lights up: the patrol splits into two teams, buzzers armed, host
on the cards, the cardboard world cup at stake.

- Buzzer round: quick-fire on the World Scout Movement, answers on the
  backs so any host sounds expert. Where did it start (Brownsea Island,
  1907)? How many scouts today (57 million, nearly every country)? Who has
  the most (Indonesia)? What does WOSM do (links national scout groups,
  runs World Jamborees, backs Messengers of Peace)? Wrong buzz, free steal.
- Future round: sixty seconds per team to pitch the one thing World
  Scouting should do next - a climate jamboree, scouts in every refugee
  camp, bolder. Double points for the boldest idea that could really work.
- Phrase-master: out come the phrase cards in a language nobody at the
  table speaks, phonetics included. Five minutes in pairs to drill hello,
  please, thank you, goodbye and "My name is..." - then each scout steps
  up and introduces themselves to the host entirely in the new language.
- Judge accent and confidence, never perfection: a point for going for it,
  two for swagger, a finger on the map for the country they borrowed from.

Scores totalled, cup lifted, and the winning team's greeting becomes the
patrol's official hello for the rest of the night.
