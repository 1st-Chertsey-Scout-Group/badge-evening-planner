Hills are not dangerous to people who can see trouble coming. Ten photos,
three scenarios and one phone call decide whether this patrol can.

- Photo blitz: ten seconds per hazard photo, patrols write every danger
  they spot - swollen river, crag edge, loose scree, mist, ridge wind,
  failing light, cold. A point per hazard named.
- Scenario round: mist down and no path; a rolled ankle; the river up since
  morning. Patrols commit to a plan aloud, then the referee's book comes
  out - Safety on Mountains, the BMC's pocket bible - to score it. Ankle
  care counts: support it, keep them warm, then walk out or call out.
- Kit interlude: pass round the group first aid kit and shelter - who
  carries them, when they come out, what everyone wears so they rarely do.
- The call, on prop phones with a leader as operator: 999, ask for police,
  then mountain rescue. Have ready: grid reference, what happened,
  casualties and injuries, group size, kit. No signal? Text 999 (register
  the phone first), or six whistle blasts, a minute's pause, repeat.
- In Touch, for real: fill the card for the troop's next walk - who at home
  holds the route and timings, the check-in times, who rings whom if the
  group is late. The best card becomes the actual plan.
