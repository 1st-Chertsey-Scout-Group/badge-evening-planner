---
title: Air spotters club
description: Buzzer-round aircraft recognition - silhouettes, national markings and what the F in F-35 means.
duration: 15
equipment:
  - Aircraft silhouette flashcards (a dozen or more types)
  - National markings cards - service roundels and civil registration prefixes for six countries including the UK
  - Two buzzers or bells
  - Printed RAF and NATO letter designation table
  - Whiteboard and pen for the scores
tags:

  - '49104'
  - '49107'
  - '49108'
---

Wartime ops room: silhouettes flash up, two teams sit on buzzers and points go
on the board - but naming an aircraft scores nothing until you say how you knew.

- Silhouette round: three seconds per card. Buzz, name it, then earn the point
  with the giveaway features - wing shape and position, engine count and
  placement, tail shape. "High wing, four props, deep belly" beats a lucky
  guess every time.
- Licence check: the leader picks six aircraft for the night and the teams
  drill them until every scout can describe all six from memory.
- Markings round: roundel side up for the service marking, flip for the civil
  registration prefix. Six countries with the UK always in the deck - RAF
  roundel and G-, US star and N, French cockade and F.
- Designations: deal the RAF and NATO letter table - F fighter, C transport,
  E electronics - then call real aircraft (F-35, C-17, E-3) and teams buzz the
  job the letter promises. Every scout gives three designations with an
  example to close the round.

Lightning finale on the full mixed deck, double points - winners name the club.
