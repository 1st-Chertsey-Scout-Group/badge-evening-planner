The dragons - two leaders, stern faces - sit behind a giant cheque. Patrols
are founders with fifteen minutes to find a cause worth funding.

- Investigate: deal the issue cards - local stories of litter, lonely older
  residents, the food bank, the flooded park. Add what the paper missed,
  pick the ONE issue that matters, and write down what you want to change.
- Plan the action: something this troop could genuinely do this term - a
  litter blitz, a food bank collection, a games afternoon at the care home.
  Who does what, where, with what kit; vague plans get eaten alive.
- Forge the opening line: one sentence that makes a stranger reach for
  their wallet - clear, true, impossible to ignore. Test it on the patrol
  next door; if they shrug, rewrite it.
- The den: two minutes per patrol in front of the whole troop, opening line
  first. Dragons grill them - what does it cost, who benefits, why you? -
  and score need, plan and that first sentence.

The winning patrol takes the cheque and the plan goes in the programme for
real - say so up front, because it changes how hard they think.
