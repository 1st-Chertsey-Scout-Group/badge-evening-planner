A line of perfectly normal drinks and snacks down the table and a box of
sugar cubes at the end: guesses first, then the truth, stacked.

- Sugar detective: for each item the patrol writes a cube guess, then stacks
  the real answer beside the packet - nine in the cola, twelve in the energy
  drink, seven hiding in the "healthy" smoothie. Closest guess takes the
  point; the towers do the arguing.
- Match day: kick-off Saturday, 10am. Build the twenty-four hours before it
  from the food and bedtime cards - proper meal the night before, real
  breakfast, water in the bag, lights out in time for nine hours; the sleep
  cards say why: reactions, mood and decisions all run on sleep and fuel.
  Judge scores each plan against the cards, not with a telling-off.
- Mythbusters: cards up one at a time on smoking, vaping, alcohol and drugs;
  the patrol calls true, false or marketing, then reads the facts off the
  back. "Vaping is just water vapour" - false, and marketing. "Alcohol warms
  you up" - false, you lose heat faster. "Nicotine is addictive however it
  arrives" - true. Point per correct call.

Keep it matter-of-fact: the cube towers and the card backs make the case,
and the scoreboard keeps it a game.
