Two of the biggest killers, and the treatment is recognition. Casualty actors
get a script; diagnosing them in under a minute is the game.

- Teach the two pictures first. Heart attack: crushing chest pain like a band,
  pain into the arm or jaw, pale, sweaty, frightened. Stroke: FAST - Face
  drooping on one side, Arm that drifts down, Speech slurred or strange,
  Time to call 999.
- Run the FAST checks on each other so they feel real: smile in the mirror,
  both arms up with eyes closed, repeat a phrase.
- Heart attack drill: sit the casualty on the floor against a wall, knees
  bent - the W position, build it with cushions - keep them still and calm,
  999, nothing to eat, ask about their own medication (some carry a spray).
- Stroke drill: any one FAST sign is enough. 999, say the word "stroke", keep
  them comfortable, note the time it started - the hospital will ask.

Then the 60-second rounds: actor performs, patrol diagnoses, calls it and
starts treatment before the stopwatch dies. Both conditions, same finish
line: minutes save heart muscle and brain.
