The newsroom is open and tonight's desk has one job: nothing gets printed
until it has been checked.

- Front desk sort: headline cards slapped into REAL and FAKE piles, fact and
  opinion cards into their own two piles, against the clock. The editor
  (leader) marks them and the patrol names the tells - no source, too good
  to be true, "everyone is saying", a feeling dressed up as a fact.
- Feed audit: each scout lists the five accounts, channels or chats they see
  most, then tallies how alike that five is - ages, genders, hobbies, takes.
  Compare with the scout next to you: nobody's feed matches, because the
  algorithm feeds you more of what you already liked. Name what that does
  to the opinions you end up believing.
- Ad detective: pairs get five minutes on a laptop to dig out one way
  algorithms or targeted ads use personal data (start at "why am I seeing
  this ad"), then turn the find into a sixty-second demo or quiz question
  and run it on the next patrol at changeover.
- Lightning debate: motion card up, desks split into FOR and AGAINST, three
  minutes on the laptops to arm up - online evidence only, and every claim
  spoken must name where it came from. Two minutes a side. The room holds
  up 1-5 scorecards for each source as it is cited: who wrote it, when,
  and why.
- Verdict beat, one minute: hands up - how easy was it to know which
  evidence to trust? Best-sourced desk wins the front page.
