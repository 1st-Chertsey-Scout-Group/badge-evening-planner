Five rings, one circuit, the scoreboard where everyone can see it. Pairs
rotate on the whistle, three minutes a ring, and one currency spends
everywhere: six knots and three lashings.

- Speed ring: reef, sheet bend, clove hitch, round turn and two half
  hitches, bowline and timber hitch, called by name and tied against the
  clock, then square, diagonal and sheer lashings on the staves. No name, no
  points.
- Blindfold ring: buff down, tie the clove hitch and bowline by feel - the
  knot you can tie blind is the one you still own in the dark on camp.
- Description ring: the caller reads the job - "join two ropes of different
  thickness", "make a line fast to a post in a hurry" - and the pair names
  the knot, ties it and says where it earns its keep.
- Rope surgery: whip a frayed end with twine until it would pass kit
  inspection, then the splice - the leader opens a back splice and every
  scout tucks a strand home.
- Pit stop: the whistle stops the clock only when every rope at the ring is
  coiled, finished and hung. Spaghetti on the floor costs five points.

Totals on the board at full time; top patrol takes the prize.
