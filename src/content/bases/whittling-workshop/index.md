---
title: Whittling workshop
description: Earn a knife licence rule by rule, learn why blunt is the dangerous one, then carve a tent peg that survives the mallet.
duration: 20
equipment:
  - Fixed-blade or locking knives, one per scout (six scouts, two leaders)
  - Cut-resistant gloves for the holding hand
  - Green or seasoned sticks, thumb-thick, two per scout
  - Sharpening stone, a drop of oil and one deliberately blunt knife for the comparison
  - First aid kit with plasters in reach
  - Tarp or logs to mark the whittling circle
tags:
  - Scouts

---

Nobody carves until they hold a licence, and licences are earned in the
circle: six scouts, two leaders, six knives counted out.

- The licence: every rule demonstrated back to a leader before the card is
  signed - sit down to carve, check the blood bubble (no one closer than
  arm plus blade), glove on the holding hand, cut away from your body, pass
  a knife closed and handle-first, never walk with an open blade.
- Blunt is the dangerous one: feel the blunt knife tear bark where the
  sharp one slices - blunt needs force, and force is what slips. Then the
  stone: bevel flat, small circles, both sides, wipe and test. Everyone
  takes strokes. Care in one breath: clean, dry, a drop of oil, never put
  away wet.
- The carve: a tent peg with a point and a guyline notch, or a toasting
  stick, peeled and pointed. Small push cuts - let the edge do the work.
- The test: pegs are mallet-driven into the field and must hold a guyline
  tug; toasting sticks earn first turn at the next campfire. The best peg
  goes into the patrol box for camp.

Knives stay seated inside the marked circle, and the base ends as it began -
six blades closed, six knives counted back.
