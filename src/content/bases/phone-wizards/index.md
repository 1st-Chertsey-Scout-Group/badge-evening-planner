Five levels, one checklist card, fifteen minutes. Clear them all and the
patrol leaves as certified phone wizards.

- Level 1, lockdown: passcode or fingerprint on, find-my-phone switched on,
  and the lost-phone drill said out loud - lock it remotely, tell your
  network, and never post your number where strangers can read it. Partner
  checks the card before the level clears.
- Level 2, jargon decoder: match the cards. SMS is plain text, MMS carries
  pictures, 3G and 4G are generations of mobile data, Bluetooth is a
  short-range radio link, WAP is how phones did the web before smartphones.
  First pair to all six calls it.
- Level 3, address books: build a contact group called Patrol on the phone
  with everyone in it, then mirror it in email - webmail on the laptop,
  school accounts count - one group, same members. Two books managed, one
  level cleared.
- Level 4, the invite: craft the most creative text, photo or
  fifteen-second video message inviting a mate to the event on the flyer.
  Patrol votes the winner, and the winner genuinely sends it.
- Level 5, publish: photograph this base in action and share the best shot
  to the troop album - the link is open on the laptop.

Troop album only, nothing public, and nobody in a photo who waves it away -
that is the wizards' code.
