The walk out went wrong and night is coming. The patrol stays where it is,
gets findable, and lives out of a tin - points in every round.

- Kit draft: thirty items, ten slots in the tin. Pairs pick, pack and defend
  every choice to the leader - whistle, foil blanket and waterproof matches
  earn points; the deodorant and phone charger get read aloud to everyone.
- Signal school: distress is six blasts or flashes in a minute, a minute of
  silence, repeat until answered; three back means heard-you-coming. Learn
  it on whistle, torch and mirror.
- Field test: half the patrol crosses the field and sends distress by
  whistle, then by torch; the other half must answer correctly to score.
  Swap and repeat.
- Stay found: stage the wait - stay put at the last known point, markers and
  foil blanket out in the open, huddle warm and off the ground, torch saved
  for signalling. The leader overflies as the search helicopter and scores
  what they can spot.
- Casualty round: a gashed shin and a turned ankle, treated from the tin and
  clothing only - plaster, folded buff as a dressing, spare sock as padding,
  casualty kept warm, dry and reassured.
