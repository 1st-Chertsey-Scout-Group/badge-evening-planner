Chalk a pit lane, flip the bikes, and run the patrol as a race crew - every
scout gets hands on every job before the stopwatch comes out.

- Brakes and gears: spin the wheel and pull the lever - pads must bite well
  before the lever reaches the bar; run the full gear range and cure any
  skipping with quarter-turns on the barrel adjusters.
- Rider fit: saddle raised until the leg is almost straight with a heel on the
  pedal, bars level with the saddle - then the helmet: two fingers above the
  eyebrows, straps in a V at the ears, one finger under the chin.
- Lights: fit the set, check both ends, swap the batteries - a dead light is a
  mechanical fault, fix it like one.
- Dropped chain drill: derail it on purpose, re-seat it on the chainring with
  two fingers, pedal forward. No drama, no oily elbows.
- The pit stop, timed: front wheel out, find the puncture, check inside the
  tyre for the thorn that caused it, patch or swap the tube, wheel back in,
  pumped hard. Fastest sound repair takes the podium.

Helmets on for any test ride, and fingers stay clear of spokes and chainrings
while wheels are spinning.
