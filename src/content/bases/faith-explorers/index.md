Open the auction house. Lots on the board: honesty, adventure, wealth,
kindness, fame, friendship. Bidding starts now.

- Values auction: pairs bid their bundle on the values they would actually
  pay for. Hammer down, each pair defends what it bought and what it let
  go - then the patrol picks the one value a Scout should never sell.
- Text table: each pair draws a quote card from a faith they don't know,
  reads it aloud and hunts for the line closest to a Scout value. Then the
  compare beat: is this near what you believe, miles from it, or a
  surprise? One sentence each, every answer allowed.
- Daily-life detective: match the practice cards - halal and kosher food,
  five daily prayers, the Sikh kara, Shabbat - to their faiths against the
  clock, then each pair talks the patrol through one believer's ordinary
  Tuesday, waking to bedtime.
- Founder's story: draw a six-beat card - Siddhartha leaving the palace,
  Guru Nanak at the river, Moses, Jesus, St George - and retell it as a
  two-minute drama, narrator and frozen scenes; audience names the moment.

Curiosity is the whole game: explorers reading other people's maps, never
judges marking them - and nobody is asked to pray, only to wonder.
