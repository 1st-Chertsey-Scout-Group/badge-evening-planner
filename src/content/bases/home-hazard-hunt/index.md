Stage a corner of the hall as the world's most dangerous lounge: washing
drying on the heater, candle under the curtain, a daisy-chain of socket
strips, matches at toddler height, party decorations on the lightshade, a
disposable BBQ "just in case". Photo cards stand in for the gas fire, the
chip pan and the open fire.

- Patrols sweep the room with clipboards: every hazard found, named, and -
  the part that scores - explained. Why does drying clothes on a heater kill?
  Where should the matches live? What's wrong with that fuse?
- Reset round: the patrol makes the room safe, including the overnight
  routine - doors and windows closed, because a shut door buys minutes.
- Smoke alarm minute: test button, where they go (every level, outside
  bedrooms), test monthly. Press it so everyone knows the sound.
- Fire breaks out anyway: shout fire, get everyone out, shut doors behind
  you, meet at the meeting point, 999 from outside. Never back in - not for
  the dog, not for the phone.

Every scout leaves with an escape plan sheet to draw their own home's two
ways out and the family meeting point - homework is talking it through with
whoever they live with.
