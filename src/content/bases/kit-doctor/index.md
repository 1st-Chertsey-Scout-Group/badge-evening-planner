A leader stands in the middle of the hall in a t-shirt, looking cold. Flip
the forecast card: winter hill, sleet by lunch. Three minutes.

- The race: half the patrol dresses the leader from the kit table, half
  packs the daysack. The table is booby-trapped. Hands off at the whistle.
- Surgery round, where the points live: every item worn or packed gets
  justified aloud - what it is for and how it is used. Whistle: six blasts,
  a minute's pause, repeat. Survival bag: get in, sit on the pack, stay
  put. Spare food stays packed - for the day going wrong, not snack o'clock.
- Layers beat one big coat: name the jobs - base wicks sweat, mid layer
  warms, shell stops wind and rain. Hats and gloves weigh nothing.
- Decoys cost double: jeans and cotton hoodies soak, freeze and never dry;
  flip flops end walks; glass breaks in a pack.
- Re-deal: new forecast, swap jobs. Heatwave wants the sun hat, suncream
  and double water; downpour wants full shells and dry socks in a bag. Add
  the group question: what do the adults carry for everyone - group first
  aid kit, group shelter, the route card?

Highest justified-item score wins; pack for the day going wrong, not the
day on the poster.
