A contour map is just a mountain fed through a bread slicer. Tonight every
pair builds the mountain and works the slicer.

- Build a fist-high mountain with all four landforms, named aloud as they
  go on: a valley (a river-cut groove running down and out), a ridge (a
  long high spine), a spur (a nose of high ground off the side) and a col
  (the saddle dip between two tops). No landform, no slicing.
- Slice it into three or four layers with the floss held dead level - rest
  it on the skewer stacks so each cut stays flat.
- Trace: bottom layer on card, draw round it; restack, trace the next layer
  inside, and so on to the summit. Lift the mountain away and there it is -
  its own contour map.
- Read it back: lines crowded together are steep ground, spread out are
  gentle. Hunt the four landforms in the lines: valley contours make Vs
  pointing uphill, spur Vs point downhill, a ridge is a long tongue of
  stacked lines, a col is the waist pinched between two rings.
- Showdown: leader calls a landform; first finger on a real one on the OS
  extract scores. Match photo cards to extracts, then swap drawings - can
  the other pair find your col and your steepest face from paper alone?
