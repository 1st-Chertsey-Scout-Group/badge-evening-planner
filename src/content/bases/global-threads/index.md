---
title: Global threads
description: Pin your own clothing labels across a world map, argue out why nothing is made here, then craft something from a country you are wearing.
duration: 15
equipment:
  - Large world map taped to the wall and sticker dots
  - Origami paper with simple fold guides (crane, jumping frog)
  - Rangoli pattern cards and coloured chalk or coloured sand trays
  - Weaving cards and wool
  - Red tissue paper and green pipe cleaners for paper poppies
tags:
  - Scouts
  - Global
  - Indoor
requirements:
  - '40992'
  - '45427'
---

Check your collar. Everyone hunts their own labels - jumper, shoes, the
water bottle in the bag - and shouts the country of origin.

- Map it: a sticker dot on every country found. Watch the cluster appear -
  Bangladesh, Vietnam, China, Turkey - and the gaping hole over the UK.
  Points for the rarest country and for anything made closest to home.
- The why round: why does a British shop make a T-shirt five thousand miles
  away? Collect answers - wages, factories, materials - then flip it: who
  wins, who loses? Cheaper clothes and new jobs against shipping miles,
  lost local trades and how the makers are treated. Keep it running.
- Hands busy: pick a craft from a pinned country and make one to take home
  - origami from Japan, a rangoli pattern from India, card weaving, paper
  poppies. Fold guides on the table; first finished helps the next.
- Gallery line at the close: best make from each table held up, and the map
  stays on the wall for the rest of term.

Everyone leaves with a thing they made and one advantage and one
disadvantage of overseas making they can say out loud.
