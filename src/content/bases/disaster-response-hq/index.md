The hall is an aid agency ops room, a disaster struck this morning, and the
cargo plane leaves tonight. The patrol is the duty crew.

- First shift: hand over the photo pack and run the news clip. Three
  minutes to pull the facts - what happened, where, who is worst hit, what
  is broken - then one scout reports it back in thirty seconds.
- Load the plane: every aid card has a cost and a weight, the sheet caps
  both, and exactly five items fly. Argue it out - does water purification
  beat blankets? Are satellite phones a luxury or the key to all the rest?
- Manifest check: defend your five to the ops chief, who challenges one
  choice. Points for reasoning that matches the coverage, not gut feeling.
- Second shift, the outbreak: draw a health issue card - malaria, Ebola,
  HIV - and five minutes to turn its facts into a sixty-second brief for
  the whole troop: bulletin, mime, jingle or poster. Facts and style score.
- Last order, whole room: agree the one thing scouts here could really do
  next time a disaster hits - collect, fundraise, raise the alarm.

Pick a disaster recent enough to remember but not one that touched families
in the room - check before the evening.
