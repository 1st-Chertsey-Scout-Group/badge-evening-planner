  - Crowbar or scaffold pole with wooden blocks for the fulcrum
  - Short log rollers (round fence posts work)
  - Work gloves

A log too heavy to lift, a line on the grass it must cross, and one rule:
muscle is banned. Machines only - bar, rollers, then a lashed crane.

- Levers first: the leader sets the stance - palms down, back straight, feet
  clear of the bar - then every scout takes a turn. Fulcrum block close to
  the log and the end floats up one-handed; slide the block away and feel it
  turn impossible. That difference is the whole machine.
- Lever the log high enough to feed a roller underneath, then walk it: roll,
  leapfrog the rear roller to the front, repeat until it crosses the line.
- Raise the crane: sheer-lash two poles near the head, splay the heels and
  pin them, guy it fore and aft, hang the pulley at the crossing and sling
  the load beneath.
- The lift runs on one voice: take the strain - haul - hold. Score the
  rescue: up smoothly, ten seconds hovering clear of the ground, down under
  control. Jerks, drops or a leaning frame lose points; re-rig and go again.

Feet and fingers never go under load or bar, and nobody touches the rig
until the load is back on the ground.
