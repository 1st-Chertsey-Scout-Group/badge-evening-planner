Cordon off a corner of the field as the worst picnic in history: gate open
by the sheep, litter drifting, the dog loose in lambing season and bike
tracks carved clean through the crops.

- Sweep: patrols work the scene as wardens, dropping a numbered marker on
  every offence and logging it. A point per offence found.
- Charge: file each offence under one of the Countryside Code's three
  headings - respect everyone, protect the environment, enjoy the outdoors -
  and name the fix: litter bagged and carried out, dog leaded near stock,
  gate closed or left as the farmer had it. Point for heading, point for fix.
- The bike corner goes to the Mountain Bike Code of Conduct: ride only where
  riding is allowed - bridleways and byways, never footpaths - no skidding
  new lines, give way to walkers and horses. Follow one skid into its
  future: rut, then channel, then gully once rain finds it.
- Habitat beat: the shortcut trampled past the log pile. Corner-cutting
  widens paths, erodes soil and wrecks the places things nest and shelter -
  each patrol names two ways to walk that preserve them.
- Reset, against the clock: put the scene right so a stranger couldn't tell
  anyone had been. Leaders judge; charges plus reset decide the win.
