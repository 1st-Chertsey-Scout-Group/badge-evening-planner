The newsroom is an hour before deadline, compressed into twenty minutes.
The story: last month's camp. The editor (a leader, red pen) buys one
release tonight.

- Know your outlet: skim the local paper and the outlet cards - who reads
  or listens to each one, and what gets in: names, faces, local places, a
  strong photo. Pick your outlet and angle the story for it; "12 local
  kids cook on open fires" beats "Scouts went camping".
- First line carries all five Ws - who, what, where, when, why. Write it,
  swap desks, and the rival patrol hunts the missing W. No W, no story.
- Build the release on the template: a headline that is not boring (the
  editor spikes "Scouts have fun" on sight), two short paragraphs, a real
  quote with name and age from a scout who was there, the proving photo.
- Deadline: copy on the editor's desk before the countdown dies; the red
  pen marks live - Ws present, headline grabs, quote sounds human. The
  winner is emailed to the district media contact and the paper tonight;
  the rest run on the group website and newsletter, so nothing is wasted.

House rule before anything leaves the building: real names checked and
photo permissions confirmed.
