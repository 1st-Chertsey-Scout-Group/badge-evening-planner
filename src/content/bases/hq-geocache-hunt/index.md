Every patrol leaves having hidden a real cache and raided someone else's.

- First, drive the unit. At the door, read where you stand and record it
  both ways on the card: six-figure grid reference for the OS map, latitude
  and longitude for the planet - and for every geocache listing.
- Programming drills: key in the card's lat/long point and follow the arrow
  to it; on arrival, pace out the unit's accuracy figure - 8m of error is a
  real search area. Then the landmark by grid reference: the unit gives a
  bearing and distance, so set the map to match and walk that bearing
  across the field, checking arrow and map agree on where the landmark sits.
- Cache law before anything is hidden (rule cards out): permission, never
  buried, never where a search pulls strangers toward traffic (Highway
  Code), land and gates left as found (Countryside Code), no digging or
  trampled habitat (GAGB guidelines). An illegal hide scores nothing.
- The duel: hide a micro cache with its log strip, mark the waypoint, write
  both coordinate forms on a swap card; trade cards with another patrol.
  First to find, sign and replace exactly as found wins - and the best
  legal hide gets written up with a leader and submitted to the site.

Everything stays inside the grounds, and nothing goes near the road.
