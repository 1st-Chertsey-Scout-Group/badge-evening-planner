title: Tree detectives

Eight numbered suspects stand around the grounds and the street outside:
every one gets named tonight, and the key is the interrogation script.

- Two minutes on one sample tree to learn the key: needles or broad leaves?
  Opposite or alternate? Lobed, toothed or smooth? Answer the questions in
  order and the key hands you the name - a flowchart, not a guess.
- Evidence run in pairs: route cards lead to eight tagged trees, deciduous
  and conifer mixed. Work the key at each, log the verdict, and bag a fallen
  leaf or take a bark rubbing as the exhibit. Two points per correct name,
  one if only the family is right.
- Cross-examination: pairs swap sheets and mark against the answer card.
  Disputed trees go back to the key, not to a shouting match.
- Forestry brief under the last tree: commercial forests are thinned, felled
  and replanted for timber; natural woodland is managed lighter - coppicing,
  deadwood left for wildlife. Threat round for bonus points: wind
  (windthrow), frost (split bark, dead young growth), fire, and the grey
  squirrel's bark-stripping habit - name each damage and one defence.

A full evidence file earns detective rank and the rubbings go up on the
noticeboard; the street leg stays on the pavement with a leader at the gate.
