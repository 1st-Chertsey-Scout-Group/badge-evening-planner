The casualty isn't breathing. This is the one base where the scouts do the
real thing, full force, on a manikin.

- Run the lead-in fast: no response, no normal breathing, 999 on speaker and
  ask for a defibrillator. Then hands on.
- Heel of the hand on the centre of the chest, fingers locked, arms straight,
  push hard and fast - a third of the chest depth, full recoil every time.
- Start the playlist: 100-120 beats per minute is the tempo. Compressions to
  the music, thirty at a time.
- Two rescue breaths through the face shield between sets - head tilt, chin
  lift, seal, watch the chest rise. Happy to give breaths? 30:2. Not? Hands-on
  CPR without stopping still saves lives.
- Two-minute swap drill: pairs trade places mid-rhythm without losing a beat,
  because real CPR exhausts a grown adult in two minutes.

Finish on the one rule that matters: any CPR beats no CPR. You cannot make an
unbreathing casualty worse.
