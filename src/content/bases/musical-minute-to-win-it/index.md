Gameshow rules: the patrol splits into two teams, points on the board,
sixty seconds a round, and the host (the leader) plays it big.

- Hum it back: the host plays or sings a two-line phrase; one scout sings
  it back solo for two points, the whole team in chorus rescues it for
  one. Start with tunes they know, then sneak in ones they don't - the ear
  does the work either way.
- Drum it back: the host plays a tune, the team claps or beats its rhythm
  back on the buckets - not the notes, the pattern. Each round adds a bar.
  Last team still locked to the beat takes the round.
- Name that tune in five notes: buzzers ready, steals allowed, smug faces
  expected. Five notes, then three for the brave.
- Desert island disc: the wooden spoon mic goes round and every scout gets
  thirty seconds with the host - the one song or artist they would keep
  forever, and why it gets them. The host listens like it matters, because
  it does; a follow-up question each ("when do you play it?") and a point
  for any pitch that makes someone else want to hear it.

Play the winning desert island song as the patrol rotates out.
