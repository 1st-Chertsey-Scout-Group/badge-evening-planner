The hall is circus school for the night: four skill stations, progression
cards from first catch to show-off, and a finale that nobody sits out.

- Tour the stations, then choose two skills from different families - one
  from juggling (scarves, then balls), one from spinning (plates) or
  manipulation (diabolo, flower sticks). No two acts from the same family.
- Drill skill one for five minutes: the cards climb a step at a time - one
  scarf, two, the full cascade; or get the plate spinning, keep it alive on
  the stick, then walk with it.
- Swap and drill skill two. Drops are part of the syllabus - pick it up and
  go again, and tick each level off the card as it lands.
- Ringmaster's interviews while hands are busy: the leader works the
  stations with the circus-life cards - life on the road, how circus
  families train, modern circuses without animals, real circus schools you
  can join - and every scout talks one card through with the leader.
- The show: mark out the ring, each scout announces their act (nose
  optional) and performs their best thirty seconds of both skills to the
  rest.

Applause decides the star turn - drops cost nothing, style is everything.
