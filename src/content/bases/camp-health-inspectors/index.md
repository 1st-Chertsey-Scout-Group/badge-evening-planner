Stage the kitchen wrong before they arrive - raw chicken over the salad,
cool box gaping, bin overflowing, washing-up bowl beside the toilet tent - a
dozen violations in all. Hi-vis on, clipboards out: the inspectors have come
about the complaints.

- The sweep: log every violation. A find scores one; naming the damage it
  does - food poisoning flattening the camp, rats in the bin - doubles it.
- Fix the food: rebuild the cool box - raw meat sealed at the bottom,
  ready-to-eat above, lid shut, box in the shade - and dry food sealed and
  off the ground. At home the fridge does this job; at camp the cool box is
  the fridge, so it stays shut.
- Fix the site: washing-up moves well away from the loos, a handwash station
  goes by the kitchen, hands washed before cooking and after the toilet -
  and the inspectors say why, or the fix doesn't count.
- Fix the rubbish: sorted for recycling, food waste sealed against animals,
  bags tied and bound for the site bins or home - never buried, never
  burned.
- Sign-off: the patrol walks the leader round the now-legal kitchen and
  defends the three worst finds. Full marks reopens the kitchen; miss the
  chicken and the site stays shut.
