---
title: Throwline rescue range
description: Shout, reach, throw and never go in - a dry-land rescue range with a drifting casualty and an ice scenario on gym mats.
duration: 20
equipment:
  - Throw bags or throwlines (two or more)
  - Buoyant aids - a rescue throw ring, plus empty 2-litre bottles and tape for improvised floats
  - Broom pole and a towel for reach rescues
  - Gym mats as the ice sheet
  - Cones and chalk to mark the bank
tags:
  - Scouts
  - Water safety
  - Outdoor
requirements:
  - '45138'
  - '45140'
---

Chalk a river across the grass, cone the bank, and set a leader drifting
slowly "downstream" as the casualty. Every rescue tonight runs from dry land.

- The rescue ladder, lowest risk first - each scout calls it and shows it.
  Shout: clear instructions and encouragement ("kick your legs, work towards
  me"). Reach: lie flat with the pole or towel, a buddy anchoring your legs.
- Throw: a buoyant aid first to keep them up - the ring, or bottles taped
  into a float - then the throwline to bring them in: underarm, land it over
  the casualty's shoulder, drag it into their hands, haul from a seated
  brace. Points per catchable throw; re-stuff the bag fast or the next throw
  tangles.
- Wade is the last resort before swimming - talked through at the chalk
  shoreline only, never in real water: pole to test depth, shuffle side-on,
  no deeper than knees. Chant the order to score: shout, reach, throw, wade.
- Ice finale on the mats: the casualty is through the "frozen lake". Nobody
  steps onto the ice - not for a person, not for a dog. 999, shout "arms on
  the edge, kick your legs flat", reach or throw from the bank, and hold a
  dog's owner back: dogs usually climb out, rescuers don't.

Never tie a throwline to yourself or wrap it round a hand - hold it, because
letting go always beats being pulled in.
