The guild is hiring. Two benches, commissions chalked on the board, and a
guild master (the leader) who stamps nothing second-rate.

- Bench one, the construction set commission: thirty seconds of design
  first - sketch or say what you are building and how it will stand - then
  build it in Lego or Meccano. Pick one: a bridge between two chairs that
  takes the house brick, or a tower past the metre mark.
- Bench two, the junk commission: raid the recycling crates for a model
  with at least one moving part - a flap, a wheel, a hinge of tape. Half
  the patrol starts at each bench and swaps midway.
- The parts patter: to get on the guild books, sort the spares tray naming
  what is what - brick, plate, Technic beam, axle, gear, Meccano strip -
  and say which other kits the system sells for the next commission.
- The stamp: every builder talks the guild master through the work - what
  the design was, which part failed first, what they would change next
  time. Honest answers earn the logbook stamp; "it just worked" earns
  another question.

Finale: bricks loaded onto bridges until one stands alone, applause verdict
for the junk builds, and the wreckage back in the tubs.
