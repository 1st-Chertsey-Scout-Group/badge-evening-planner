Fog has swallowed the coast and the skipper wants one answer: where are we?
Every fix is scored against the leader's secret position - millimetres here
are miles at sea, and the whole watch is stood ashore at the chart table.

- Rose warm-up: a mariner's compass reads in degrees and in 32 points of
  11.25 each. Quickfire both ways - "ENE?" wants a number, "247?" wants a
  point.
- Two-line fix: lay position lines from the card's two charted marks; the
  ship sits where they cross. The leader measures every patrol's miss.
- Cocked hat: three cross bearings make a triangle, not a point - that is the
  cocked hat. Plot one, then use it safely: assume the corner nearest danger,
  never the hopeful middle.
- Running fix: one lighthouse left. Take a bearing, "steam" six charted miles
  on a steady course, take a second bearing, slide the first line along the
  run - where they cross is the fix.
- Compass surgery: card bearings arrive as compass readings - add easterly
  variation and deviation, subtract westerly, to plot in true; reverse the
  sums to hand the helm a compass course. Variation lives on the chart rose;
  deviation you avoid - a phone held by the hand-bearing compass drags the
  needle, so steel and speakers stay clear.

Closest combined fixes take the master navigator's cap.
