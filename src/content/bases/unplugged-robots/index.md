A leader stands behind a table of bread, butter and jam and announces they
are a robot that does exactly - EXACTLY - what the program says.

- Program v1: the patrol writes the jam sandwich instructions line by line,
  then watches the robot execute with malicious literalness. "Put jam on
  the bread" lands the whole jar on the loaf; "spread the butter" with no
  knife mentioned gets fingers. Let the chaos teach.
- Debug: circle the lines that failed, rewrite them tight - pick UP the
  knife, open the jar FIRST - and run v2. Score the patrol on the fewest
  instructions that still produce an edible sandwich.
- Land the idea in one line: that is all a computer is - a machine that
  follows instructions exactly, with no imagination and no "you know what
  I mean".
- Hidden computers race, sixty seconds: list every machine at home or on
  the street secretly running instructions - washing machine, video
  doorbell, traffic lights, bank card, the car (which hides dozens). Point
  apiece, bonus for the weirdest, and each find must say what job it makes
  easier.
- Pixel studio: screens draw everything as a grid of coloured squares, so
  design like a screen does - a game character (or title screen) on
  squared paper, one filled square at a time. Gallery vote picks the star
  of the patrol's imaginary game.

Only the robot handles the knife, and check allergies before anyone eats
the test output.
