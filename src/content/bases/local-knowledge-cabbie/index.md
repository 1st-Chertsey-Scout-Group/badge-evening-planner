London cabbies train for years to pass The Knowledge. Patrols get fifteen
minutes to pass the local version, with a leader in the flat cap examining.

- The Points: the examiner calls services, pairs race to slap a dot on the
  map - doctor, dentist, fire station, police station, garage, chip shop,
  park, place of worship, main bus stops, the railway station. Point per
  dot; with six down, each pair talks the quickest route from HQ to one.
- The Fare: draw a card - a passenger wants the landmark 8km away. Talk
  them there from HQ turn by turn: road names, lefts and rights, a landmark
  at every decision. Then the same fare by public transport - which bus or
  train, from which stop. The examiner is a stranger: any "turn at the
  shop, sort of" loses the fare.
- The Long Haul: out to the map edges. Which big local roads feed the
  motorway and A-road network, and which cities do they serve? Where can
  you get to from the mainline station, the coach station, the nearest
  airport - and how do you reach each of those from HQ?
- Tiebreak picture round: landmark photos go up - name it and have a finger
  on it inside ten seconds.

Top fare total wins the cap for the closing circle.
