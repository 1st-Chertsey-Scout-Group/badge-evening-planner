The base is a 999 control room. Caller and operator sit back to back so
everything has to travel by voice, like a real call.

- The operator works from a script card: which service do you need, exact
  location, what's happened, how many casualties, stay on the line.
- The caller knows only what their scenario card says - a chip-pan fire gets
  the fire service, a collapsed dog walker gets an ambulance.
- The rest of the patrol provide low-level background chaos. The operator's
  job is to keep the caller calm and on the line until help "arrives".
- Score each call out of ten for location, clarity and calm, then swap roles
  until everyone has made and taken a call.

Close on why calling early beats heroics: the call IS first aid, and sending a
bystander to phone while you help is the strongest move there is.
