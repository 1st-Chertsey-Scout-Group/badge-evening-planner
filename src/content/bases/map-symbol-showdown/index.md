Maps are a picture language and tonight is the vocabulary test, played with
the key open - using the key is map reading, not cheating.

- Warm-up on the leaflet maps: find "you are here", then fastest finger to
  the toilets, the cafe, first aid, the exit. A theme park map is an OS
  sheet with cartoon penguins.
- Symbol pictionary: one scout draws an OS symbol on the whiteboard, their
  patrol guesses; flashcard snap settles the experts. Keys stay open.
- Scavenger hunt on the Explorer sheet - but set it to north first with the
  compass (or by lining its roads up with the real one outside). Then first
  finger wins: a church with a tower, a campsite, a bridleway, a pub, a
  viewpoint.
- Orienteering map round: same ground, different language - colour does the
  work. White is runnable forest, yellow open ground, green means fight,
  blue is wet. Match five flashcards to the printed map.
- String and scale: on the Explorer 4cm is 1km on the ground; on the
  Landranger, 2cm. String a lane route between two villages, measure,
  convert - then the payoff: at 5km/h walking and 15km/h riding, how long
  to get there? Closest call takes the round.
