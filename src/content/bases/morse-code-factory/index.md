Before any message can fly, the patrol has to build the transmitter.

- Build the rig: battery to buzzer to paper-clip key - two drawing pins hold
  the clip on the card, press down to close the circuit. A dit is a tap, a
  dah is three taps long. Test rig on SOS: dit-dit-dit, dah-dah-dah,
  dit-dit-dit.
- Procedure school, two minutes: every transmission opens with callsigns -
  "Eagle station, this is Fox station, over" - "over" hands the air across,
  QSL confirms receipt, "out" closes the contact. Nobody ever says "over
  and out".
- On air: half the patrol carries a rig to the far end of the hall. Send a
  short scouting word in morse with full procedure both ends: call up, send
  slow, receiver copies onto paper and comes back QSL - or QRS, send
  slower. Swap roles. A point per word copied clean.
- The waiting half trains between transmissions: Q code top trumps until
  every scout can define eight cold - QTH where are you, QRM interference,
  QRZ who is calling me - then the phonetic relay, spelling CAMPSITE down
  the line Alpha-Bravo style against the stopwatch, every letter in proper
  phonetics.

Fastest clean message plus eight Q codes defined earns the patrol its
station licence.
