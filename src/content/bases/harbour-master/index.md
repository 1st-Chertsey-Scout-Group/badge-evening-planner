Lay a winding channel down the hall: red can buoys to port, green cones to
starboard, cardinal marks guarding a wreck. The floor is the sea tonight -
this harbour runs entirely dry.

- Buoyage briefing: entering from seaward, red cans stay to port ("is there
  any red port left?"), green cones to starboard; cardinals name the safe
  side of a danger, and leading marks lined up hold you in the channel.
- Blindfold pilotage: one scout helms blindfolded, the patrol cons them
  through by voice alone. Touch a buoy and you are aground - fewest
  groundings takes the harbour.
- Chart table: match symbols to meanings - rocks, wrecks, lights, anchorages
  - then land the catch: depths measure from chart datum, the lowest the
  tide ever gets, so the chart shows the least water you will find.
- Tide puzzle: the boat needs two metres over the bar. Read times and heights
  from the tide table, name the earliest safe crossing, then pick the hour
  whose tidal stream atlas arrows carry you in rather than fight you.
- Log round: the marine log counts miles run through the water. Deal the
  cards - log 12.4 at 1000, 18.4 at 1100 means six miles run at six knots -
  then race three more pairs for points.
