The factory floor opens: three props drawn blind from the box, one starter
card, and fifteen minutes until the sketch ships - finished or not.

- Write from scratch: the starter card sets the scene and all three props
  must appear - set-up, gag in the middle, punchline inside two minutes.
  Original material only; the audience has seen the internet too.
- Crew up: every scout draws a job card - actors, director, props master,
  sound effects, front of house. No passengers; the director's first job
  is checking everyone has one.
- Rehearse twice: once script-in-hand, once full speed with props and
  effects hitting their cues. One director's note between runs, front of
  house drafts the thirty-second intro, slot chalked on the running order.
- Showtime at flag-down: performed for the whole troop, front of house
  selling it first. The applause-meter crowns the winner and the winning
  sketch opens the next campfire.

House rule on every starter card: punchlines punch at situations, never at
scouts in the room.
