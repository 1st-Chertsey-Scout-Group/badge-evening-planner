title: Catapult workshop
description: Lash a working catapult from canes and bombard the scoring zones - once the range rules are word-perfect.
duration: 20
equipment:
  - Garden canes or broom handles, six to eight per patrol
  - Sisal or string cut into lashing lengths, plus heavy-duty elastic bands
  - Sturdy plastic serving spoons, one per patrol
  - A bag of marshmallows for ammunition
  - Hoops or buckets for scoring zones and masking tape for the firing line

An artillery workshop with one iron law: nothing fires until the range
officer has heard the patrol's safety rules - and the why behind every one.

- The build: square-lash an A-frame of canes, diagonal-lash the bracing,
  then bind on the spoon arm with a bundle of elastic bands for the snap.
  Any design qualifies if it stands on its own and throws on stored tension.
- Tension is the lesson: the lashing that fails is the loose one, on a
  marshmallow thrower or a monkey bridge alike - so every joint gets
  squeezed and re-frapped before the range opens.
- The licence: the patrol briefs the range officer before ammo is issued -
  load and fire from the line only, nobody downrange ever, lashings checked
  between volleys - and every rule must arrive with its because.
- The shoot: marshmallows at hoops two, three and four metres out for five,
  ten and twenty points, three volleys per scout. The range officer docks
  points on the spot for any breach.

Marshmallow ammunition only, the range faces an empty wall, and a loaded
catapult never leaves the firing line.
