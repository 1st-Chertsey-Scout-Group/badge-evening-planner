Flat, smooth, swept tarmac, cones out, boards in a line: a beginners' street
session run like a proper skatepark class.

- Tech check first, in pairs with the skate tool: trucks snug, wheels spin
  free with no flat spots, grip tape solid, every pad strap bites. Adjust or
  fix whatever fails - nothing rolls until its rider calls it safe, and the
  same check opens every session you ever ride.
- Falling before rolling: on the mats, drill the drop - crouch low, take it
  on the knee pads, chin tucked. Low and slow saves wrists.
- Push and glide between the cones, then controlled stops - foot drag for
  boards, heel brake for skates - until stopping is a skill, not a crash.
- Slow slalom to finish: weave the cones judged on control, not speed; the
  patrol scores style points for the smoothest run.
- Kit surgery and the where question: dry wheels after rain, rotate them;
  flat-spotted wheels, a cracked deck or a helmet after one big hit are end
  of life - replace, don't ride. Then where: skateparks and traffic-free
  tarmac with permission, never roads - name local spots that welcome it.

Hard rule: helmet and full pads on before wheels touch tarmac - no pads, no
ride, leaders included.
