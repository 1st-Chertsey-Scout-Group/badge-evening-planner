Golf with no ball: each numbered cane is a hole, the card gives a bearing
and a distance, and your feet are the club. Score is paces from the cane.

- Tee talk: the card's bearings are grid - the map kind - but the needle
  follows magnetic north: apply the printed difference before every hole.
  Hole six arrives magnetic and is logged as grid, so it runs both ways.
- Calibrate on the 50m line - your double-pace count is your ruler - then
  play the six holes: set the dial, needle in its box, sight a tuft or post
  dead on line, walk to it, repeat. Stop on your distance and count the gap.
- The boxed hole: the gazebo sits on the line. Add 90 and pace clear, walk
  the original bearing past it, subtract 90 and pace the same count back on
  line. Sloppy side-counts come straight out as strokes.
- Hole out on a back bearing: sight the cane you just left - it should read
  your bearing give or take 180. If it doesn't, you drifted: say so and
  correct before the next tee.
- Lost golfer, sprung mid-round: "where are you, exactly?" Bearings to two
  features you know - gate, flagpole - cross where you stand. Mark each
  pair's estimate; the closest takes two strokes off.

Lowest total wins the green jacket: a leader's hi-vis, worn all evening.
