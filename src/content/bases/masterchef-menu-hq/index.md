Each patrol is a catering firm bidding for the summer camp contract: feed
six people from Friday supper to Sunday lunch on thirty pounds. The leaders
are the panel, and the panel only buys what it believes.

- Draft the menu on the grid: every main meal balanced - something starchy,
  something protein, something that grew - with breakfasts that survive a
  camp morning.
- Quantities are where bids die: work out real amounts from the price lists
  (how much pasta actually feeds six?) and cost the lot. Waste rule: any
  pack opened must be finished somewhere on the menu, or the panel docks
  the bid.
- Curveball cards land mid-draft: a vegetarian, a coeliac and a nut allergy
  join the trip. Adapt the menu so everyone eats the same good food - a
  lonely special plate loses points, and the nut allergy rewrites the snack
  list, not just dinner.
- Plan the shop: the big order bought ahead, fresh bits picked up en route,
  raw meat and dairy in the cool box - freeze what you can, it's the ice
  pack until Saturday - tins and dry goods in the cupboard box. Pack the
  prop boxes to prove the plan works.
- The pitch: two minutes to the panel covering menu, costs, curveballs and
  the shopping run. The winning bid takes the contract - and cooks it for
  real at the next camp.
