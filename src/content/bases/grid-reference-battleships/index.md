Half the patrol each side, the same OS sheet each, a screen between. The
quiet villages on the map are about to become a naval theatre.

- Fence the battle zone: both sides ring the same 5x4 block of grid
  squares. Learn the call: eastings first - the numbers along the bottom -
  then northings up the side. Along the corridor, then up the stairs; four
  figures name one whole kilometre square.
- Place the fleet: four ships per side, each anchored on a real point
  feature in the zone - church, bridge, pub, trig pillar - and each written
  up as a six-figure reference: the third and sixth digits cut the square
  into tenths, pinning the ship to 100m.
- Salvos, in turns: call a four-figure square. Any enemy ship inside it:
  "splash - close!". Empty: "open water".
- Kill shots: after a splash, call all six figures to sink. Kills get
  checked with the romer - corner on the feature, tenths read straight off
  its scale. An eyeballed call a digit out is a miss, and the defender
  proves it with the romer in front of everyone.

First fleet sunk loses. Every dispute goes to the romer, and the romer is
always right.
