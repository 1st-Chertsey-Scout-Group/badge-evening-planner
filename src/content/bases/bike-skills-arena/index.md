Build the arena across tarmac, grass and gravel: chalk gate, tightening cone
slalom, slow-race lane, rough back leg. Nobody starts until their helmet
passes the gate check - two fingers above the eyebrows, V at the ears, one
finger snug under the strap.

- Timed slalom: clean through the cones, penalties for a clipped cone or a
  foot down; the slow-race lane flips it - last across without a dab wins.
- Sign cards mid-lap: a leader flashes Highway Code signs at the halfway cone
  - stop, give way, no entry, turn left - and the rider obeys on the spot. A
  misread sign costs five seconds.
- Back leg: tarmac gives way to grass and gravel - gears down, weight back,
  loose grip, let the bike move underneath you.
- Skid science between laps: skid a tennis ball across dry concrete, mark the
  stop, soak the slab and skid again. Less grip wet - same for car tyres, so
  stopping distances balloon and riders give traffic extra room.
- Kit parade at the finish: each rider names one dark-or-wet-weather
  precaution - lights front and rear, reflectors, bright layers, slower in,
  braking earlier.

Fastest clean lap takes the arena; helmets stay on from first push to last.
