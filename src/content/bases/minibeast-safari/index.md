The grounds are the savannah and the patrol are the rangers: long grass,
bushes and the log pile all hold big game if you hunt at the right scale.

- Safari code first, two minutes: before anything is touched the patrol
  writes its own rules - roll logs gently and lay them back exactly, one
  beast per pot, soft hands, release everything where it was caught, leave
  the habitat as you found it. Pin it up; breaking it costs a point.
- The safari, eight minutes: sweep-net the long grass, hold a white tray
  under a bush and tap, lift the log. Pot finds with the pooters, name them
  against the charts, tally every species on the record sheet.
- Compare the counts: mown lawn versus long grass versus log pile - which
  held the most life and why? That gap is mowing and tidying deciding who
  gets to live here, and the case for leaving wild corners.
- Star find: pick the patrol's best beast and work it up from the fact cards
  - what it eats, what eats it, where it hides, its party trick.
- Ranger's talk: sixty seconds presenting the star find, pot held high, to
  the others - then release it exactly where it came from.

One-next-thing round as the pots empty: everyone names one thing they will
do next - bug hotel, wild patch, an ID book. Hands washed before snacks.
