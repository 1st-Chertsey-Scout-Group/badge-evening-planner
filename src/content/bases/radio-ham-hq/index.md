How does a whisper cross the planet with no wires? Start with a skipping
rope and end at the ionosphere.

- Wave demo: two scouts stretch the rope, one wiggles - that travelling
  ripple is a radio wave, and faster wiggling means higher frequency. VHF
  (the 2 metre band) beams straight like a torch: fine across town, gone
  past the horizon. HF (the 80, 40 and 20 metre bands) cheats - it bounces
  off the ionosphere, an electric mirror 100 km up, and skips around the
  curve of the Earth. Patrol explains it back with the rope before moving
  on.
- Callsign bingo: prefix cards stuck on the map - G and M are UK, EI
  Ireland, F France, DL Germany, PA Netherlands, ON Belgium, EA Spain. The
  leader barks made-up callsigns ("F4ABC!") and patrols slap the right
  country. Run it until UK and the near continent are reflex.
- Greetings message: read the sample, then each pair drafts its own the
  proper way - callsign, name, location, a signal report, the weather, and
  73 (best wishes) to close - and reads it over the cardboard microphone.
- Licence round, true or false from the factsheet: you must pass an exam to
  transmit (true - Foundation first), Ofcom issues your callsign (true),
  you can DJ music to the world (false), you can charge money for messages
  (false), you must identify with your callsign (true).

Most bingo slaps plus a clean greetings message wins the patrol its
imaginary callsign for the rest of the night.
