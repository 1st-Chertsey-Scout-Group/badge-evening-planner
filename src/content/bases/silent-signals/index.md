Everything at this base says something without a word spoken out loud.

- Fingerspelling bootcamp: BSL cards out. The vowels live on the
  fingertips - thumb is A, then E, I, O, U along the fingers - so drill
  those first, then spell your own name until your partner reads it first
  try.
- Main transmission: draw a memory card - best moment at camp, worst
  weather on a hike - and sign a short sentence about that experience to
  your partner, fingerspelling the key words. Partner decodes out loud,
  then swap. Both decoded clean scores the pair.
- Texting time trial: the test sentence goes up - just over the magic 50
  characters. One minute on the stopwatch to type it into notes dead
  accurately; typos void the run. Fifty clean characters inside the minute
  earns the speed stamp, fastest perfect run takes the title.
- Decoder duel: abbreviation cards dealt face up - NGL, BRB, TBH, IRL,
  SMH, IDK, FYI, GOAT - patrol defines them against the leaders' attempt.
  The leaders will lose badly. Let them.

Three ways to talk with no voice at all - and one of them works across a
silent campsite after lights out.
