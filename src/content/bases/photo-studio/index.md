The studio takes on apprentices tonight: shot list on the wall, kit bench
laid out, and the night's best before-and-after owns the big screen.

- Kit bench induction, hands on every control: tap to focus, drag the
  exposure slider, flash on-off-auto. Then the two big levers - fast
  shutter freezes a jumping scout, slow streaks them; wide aperture melts
  the background, narrow keeps the whole hall sharp. Demo on a volunteer.
- Tool talk: pass the accessories tray and name what each item buys you,
  then studio rules recited back - strap or two hands, lens cloth never a
  sleeve, keep it dry, battery charged and card cleared before a shoot.
- The shoot, eight minutes, four frames per pair: action, a lamp-lit
  portrait, a tiny detail, low light without flash (tripod or braced elbows).
- Edit bay: pick your best two and work them - crop, straighten, lift the
  shadows, fix exposure and contrast. Before-and-after on the big screen:
  say what changed and why it is better; applause crowns the winner.
- Fault clinic: deal the fail photos and diagnose - shake (brace it or
  tripod it) or subject movement (faster shutter, or pan with the runner)?
  Underexposed, washed-out grey, or just backlit? Name fault, name fix.

Every pair goes home with a before-and-after - proof the eye improved.
