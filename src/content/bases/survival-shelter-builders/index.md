The storm arrives in fifteen minutes and it is carrying a watering can.
Build a shelter two of the patrol can sleep dry in, from a tarp, cord and
whatever the site gives.

- Draw a design card - lean-to, A-frame ridge or plough-point wedge - and
  read the ground first: back to the wind, never in a dip, nothing dead
  hanging overhead.
- Build: ridge line drum-tight before anything else, tarp pitched steep so
  rain runs off instead of pooling, every corner guyed and pegged,
  groundsheet in, edges banked with debris if the site allows.
- The trading post: extra pegs and cord are bought with hygiene answers
  while you build - hands cleaned before food even when water is short, the
  latrine dug downhill and well away from shelter and drinking water, water
  kept covered, dry socks saved for night. Right answer, kit handed over.
- The storm: two scouts in, watering can along the ridge and across the
  door, then a firm tug on the ridge and every corner. Dry occupants and a
  standing shelter score; drips and flapping corners cost.

Debrief at the cards: wedge nose-first into a gale, lean-to when you want a
fire in front, A-frame for a night of rain - the right shelter is the one
the weather picks.
