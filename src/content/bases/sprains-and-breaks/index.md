A&E triage desk: casualties limp in with scenario cards - twisted ankle on
the wide game, wrist that cracked on the skate ramp - and the patrol decides:
sprain or break?

- The honest answer: outside a hospital you often can't tell. Swollen and
  sore but usable leans sprain; deformed, grating, can't-put-weight-on-it,
  or a heard snap leans break. In doubt, treat as a break.
- Sprain drill - RICE: Rest, Ice (wrapped, never bare), Comfortable support,
  Elevation on the cushion stack.
- Break drill: keep it still in the position found, support above and below,
  pad around it, nothing forced straight, no food or drink in case of
  surgery, 999 for legs and anything badly out of shape.
- Sling clinic: the arm sling and the elevated sling with triangular
  bandages, then the finals - same slings improvised from neckers, jumpers
  and a belt, judged on comfort and whether the casualty can escape it.

Rotate casualty and medic until both slings are automatic. Bonus round: the
casualty rates their treatment out of ten, bedside manner included.
