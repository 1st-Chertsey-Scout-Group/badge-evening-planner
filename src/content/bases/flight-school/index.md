The hall is sky and the swivel chairs are aircraft: ground school first, then
check rides.

- Needle watch: every pair holds a compass flat and lets the needle settle.
  It is a magnet swinging to magnetic north - that is the whole machine.
- Three norths: tape three strings out from one point on the floor map - true
  north to the pole, grid north up the map's gridlines, magnetic north where
  the needle says. Name all three to pass ground school.
- Check rides: pilot in the chair, compass in hand, tower calls "turn onto
  090... 270... 180" and the pilot spins onto each heading. Fastest accurate
  turns earn wings; then scouts take over the tower.
- Sabotage the instrument: park a phone next to a compass and watch the
  needle lie. That is deviation - the aircraft's own metal and electrics
  tugging the needle - and why a cockpit compass carries a correction card.
  Each pilot explains it back in one sentence.
- Globe building: draw the equator and prime meridian on the beach ball,
  latitude rings above and below, longitude lines pole to pole; every scout
  then draws the lat/long grid on card - an address for anywhere on Earth.

Wings go only to pilots who can fly a heading and name all three norths.
