Two walls: HOT and COLD. A symptom card goes up - pale and clammy, shivering
and slurring, dizzy with cramps, clumsy and weirdly quiet - and the patrol
runs to the wall that matches. Wrong wall does press-ups.

- Build the two pictures from the cards as you go. Heat exhaustion: pale,
  sweaty, headache, dizzy, cramping. Hypothermia: the umbles - mumbles,
  stumbles, fumbles, grumbles - and shivering that later, dangerously, stops.
- Treatment relay, hot casualty: into the shade, lie them down, legs up, sips
  of water, cool the skin. Treatment relay, cold casualty: out of the wind,
  insulate from the ground, dry layers, hat on, huddle - the wrap race with
  mats, sleeping bag and foil blanket is the crowd-pleaser.
- Escalation cards end the round: hot, dry, confused casualty is heatstroke -
  999 now. Shivering that stops while they get sleepier - 999 now.

Close on where this actually happens to them: summer hikes and winter camps,
and how to spot it in a mate before the casualty stage.
