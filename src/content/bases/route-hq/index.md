Pin the brief up: a 20km hill route, start and finish fixed by a leader.
Bureaus compete for the contract; the winning card runs the next hike.

- Tools first, choice justified: Landranger (1:50000) to see the whole day,
  Explorer (1:25000) for walls, crags and field detail, road atlas and
  timetables to reach the start at all.
- Cut the route into legs that end at unmissable features. String each leg
  and convert by scale to kilometres; count the contour lines crossed going
  up - 10m of climb apiece - and log distance and climb per leg.
- Time it by Naismith's rule - 5km/h plus a minute per 10m of climb - then
  slow it honestly for a loaded patrol. Add hourly rest stops and lunch at
  a named sheltered spot; total the day and check it beats dark.
- Escape routes: every leg gets its bail-out - the nearest road, village or
  safe short way down when weather or an ankle ends the day. A leg with no
  escape gets redrawn.
- Timetables close the loop: which bus or train reaches the start, which
  brings the group home, latest safe finish worked backwards onto the card.
- The audit: bureaus swap cards, re-string one leg and re-time another.
  Arithmetic faults cost; the cleanest card wins the contract.
