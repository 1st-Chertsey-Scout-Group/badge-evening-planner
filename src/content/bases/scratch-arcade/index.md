The troop needs gadgets - a patrol point scorer, a fair name picker, a
reaction timer - and this patrol is the dev team.

- Two-minute demo of the example project: one sprite, one stack of blocks,
  one click that makes something happen. That is the whole trick;
  everything else is stacking.
- Pairs grab a laptop and a gadget card from the menu. Point scorer: "when
  sprite clicked, change score by 1". Name picker: "pick random" from a
  list of the patrol. Reaction timer: wait a random time, flash GO, let
  "timer" catch the click. The cards hint the key blocks; the build is
  theirs.
- House rule: at least one sprite is yours - drawn or redrawn in the paint
  editor and animated, flipping costumes or reacting when clicked. No bare
  grey cats ship from this factory.
- Try it for real before calling it done: score an actual point, pick an
  actual scout, time an actual leader's reactions. If it breaks, that is
  the job - debug and ship.
- Arcade time, final five minutes: rotate the laptops and play every
  machine. Sticky-note score out of ten: does it work, does it look good,
  is it fun.

Machine of the night gets used for real at flag break next week.
