Four things have gone wrong in the building at once and the patrol is the
emergency call-out crew - but this site runs to building-site rules.

- Site induction: flash the hazard cards - toxic, flammable, irritant,
  electrical danger, slippery surface. One card gates each job; the crew
  names it and picks the matching PPE from the box before clocking in.
  Wrong pick and the foreman (leader) sends you back.
- Mains hunt: find HQ's real stopcock, gas shut-off and consumer unit and
  tag each one - in a real emergency, water, gas and power go off first.
- Consumer unit drill: pick out the breaker for the "faulty" circuit, shout
  "going off" so everyone in the building knows, switch it, and tape a WORK
  IN PROGRESS tag across it so nobody flicks it back on you mid-job.
- Call-outs, three minutes each, foreman signs off every fix: plunge the
  sink rig until it runs; bind the dripping pipe with self-amalgamating tape
  and a jubilee clip; swap the dead bulb - off, cool, right wattage; cure
  the dropped hinge - tighten, matchstick the stripped hole, drop of oil.

All four jobs stamped, the right PPE every time and all three mains tagged
wins the contract. Nothing on this base is live or under pressure, and
breakers move only with a leader alongside.
