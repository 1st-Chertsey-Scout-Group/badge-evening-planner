Start with the science under glass: three jars, three candles, jars down
together - smallest dies first. Fire is a triangle of heat, fuel and oxygen,
and everything at this base kills one corner of it.

- Smoke talk while the jars fog: smoke and heat rise, the air lives near the
  floor, so in smoke you get low and go.
- Extinguisher match-up: fire cards dealt face up, extinguisher cards in
  hand, patrols snap the right pair. The traps teach the lesson - water on a
  chip pan and the leader makes the fireball noise; CO2 for the laptop;
  nothing but a fire blanket over the pan.
- Clothes on fire: stop, drop, cover your face, roll - drilled as a relay
  wearing the "burning" bib until the roll is instinct, then smother a
  volunteer leader with the fire blanket properly.
- Campfire corner: the same triangle at camp - clear ground, water bucket
  before lighting, never leave it, drown-stir-drown to kill it.

Candles are leader-handled, hair tied back, and the demo table stays between
flames and scouts.
