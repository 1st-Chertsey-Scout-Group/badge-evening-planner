Motor racing for canvas: qualifying decides where you would pitch, the race
decides whether you can - and only the crew chief may speak.

- Qualifying: rank the photo cards from dream pitch to disaster and name the
  catch in each - the slope that rolls sleepers, the flood line by the
  stream, the dead branch overhead, the bare ridge with no shelter. Flat,
  drained and sheltered wins; points for the order and the reasons.
- Lights out: one scout takes crew chief and becomes the only voice on the
  team. Pitch the tent inside the marked box, door away from the wind,
  while everyone else supplies silent hands. The clock runs from the
  whistle.
- Scrutineering: the leader walks the tent - canvas taut, pegs in at
  forty-five degrees, every guy tensioned, door out of the wind. Ten penalty
  seconds per fault.
- Pit stop: strike and pack to finish - poles and pegs counted into their
  bags, tent folded to fit, and the clock stops only when the bag closes
  and the peg count tallies.
- Podium on combined time minus penalties; swap crew chiefs and run heat
  two if the evening allows.
