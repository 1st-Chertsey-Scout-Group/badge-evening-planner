Three tricks of the scouting trade on one table: a candle that suffocates, a
compass that lies, and a foil kayak that refuses to sink. All of it is
science, and tonight this patrol are the buskers who sell it.

- Lab tour first, five minutes, leader-run. Jars down over three tea lights
  together and stopwatch the order they die: fire eats oxygen, biggest jar
  lasts longest - which is why you blow on embers and leave air gaps in a
  fire lay.
- Compass on the table: a magnetised needle lining up with the Earth's
  magnetic field. Slide the bar magnet close and watch it lie - the reason a
  compass lives away from phones, knives and tent poles.
- Foil kayak in the bowl: fold a flat-bottomed boat and load it with coins.
  It floats by pushing water out of the way, and a wide hull pushes more -
  why a loaded kayak sits lower but still rides. Most coins before sinking
  wins.
- Each pair claims a demo and gets three minutes with a busker card to plan
  the show: a hook line, the demo, the science in one sentence, one question
  to catch the audience out.
- Showtime: busk it to the rest of the patrol - or the incoming patrol at
  changeover. The audience scores fingers out of five for "I actually get it
  now". Highest average busk takes the night.

Tea lights are leader-lit and stay on the tray; hair tied back, sleeves up.
