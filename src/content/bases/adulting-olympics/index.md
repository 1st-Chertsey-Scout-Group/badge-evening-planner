Seven events, one arena: the skills nobody teaches you until the shirt is
already ruined. Scouts rotate singly or in pairs, two to three minutes an
event; leaders judge each performance with 1-10 cards, medals at flag-down.

- Hot bench: iron the uniform shirt to parade standard - collar, cuffs, one
  crease per sleeve - judged against the reference shirt on the hanger.
- Wet bench: wash up the crate spotless and dry - glasses first, pans last,
  judge runs a finger round the mugs - then bring the stove's hob back to
  gleaming with cream cleaner and elbow.
- Fiddly bench: sew the button back on so it survives the judge's tug test,
  then draw a freestyle card and nail it - fold the fitted sheet, sort a
  wash load by its care labels, make a bed with proper corners.
- Technical bench: change the bulb in the lamp and in the ceiling pendant -
  switch off, unplug or isolate, let the old one cool, match the wattage -
  then program the heating timer for a school week: warm for early mornings
  and evenings, off through the empty daytime, thermostat set for the season
  not for the tropics.

The iron gets its own table, flex out of the walkway, parked on its heel,
one scout at a time - and the pendant board is never wired to the mains.
