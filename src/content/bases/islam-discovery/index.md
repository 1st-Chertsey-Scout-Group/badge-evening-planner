Four corners of the hall, four minutes each, pairs rotating - a whole world
to discover before the dates come out.

- Pillars corner: a triple-match race - five pillars, their plain meanings
  and what each looks like in a real week, from prayer five times a day to
  daylight fasting in Ramadan. Fastest correct grid wins the corner.
- Sound corner: play the Athaan twice. Spoken or sung? Any instruments?
  Which phrase keeps returning? (Allahu Akbar - God is greatest.) It calls
  five times a day - tot up how much of a day it quietly organises.
- Inventors corner: bet on which inventions came from the Muslim world -
  coffee, the camera, algebra, hospitals, the university. Reveal: all of
  them. Match each to its inventor on the card backs to claim the points.
- Myth wall: read each card, vote true or false, flip for the fact. Most
  Muslims are Arabs? False - four in five are not; Indonesia tops the list.
- Finish together with the crossing game: step across the line if you have
  ever been the new kid, left out, or judged before anyone knew you.
  Everyone crosses - so be the one who says the first hello. Close with
  dates to taste (check allergies first), the food that breaks the fast.

Questions stay open all night - cards carry the facts, scouts the curiosity.
