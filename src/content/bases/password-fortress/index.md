Three locked prize boxes, and every combination is guessable from the
owner's profile card pinned to the lid. If this patrol can crack them,
anyone can.

- Crack the boxes: the profile cards are the owners' socials, basically -
  dog's name, birth year, lucky number, all public. Race to deduce all
  three combinations. The prizes make the point better than a lecture.
- Fortify: build the uncrackable version - three random words mashed
  together beats l3tt3r swaps every time, because longer is exponentially
  harder. Every scout drafts a practice passphrase and rates it against the
  strength cards. House rules called as you go: one per account, share with
  no one, and the real thing gets made at home with a trusted adult.
- Scenario showdown: deal the chat cards - the group-chat pile-on, the
  "keep our chats secret" stranger, the dodgy dare. Patrol must table three
  positive plays per card before the timer: back the target up, refuse to
  forward it, block and report. "Tell a trusted adult" scores double every
  time, because it works every time.
- Production split: half the patrol makes the troop backup card - what to
  do and where help lives when something online feels wrong: trusted
  adult, in-app report button, Childline 0800 1111. The other half types
  the Scout Law, online edition, on the laptop - all seven laws translated
  for the internet: trustworthy means no fake profiles, friendly and
  considerate covers the group chat.

Best backup card gets printed for every scout; the digital Law goes out on
the troop newsletter.
