Hoops at 5, 10 and 15 metres, rods rigged with hookless plugs: a casting
range on the field, an angler's licence to earn, most points takes top rod.

- Casting ladder: gentle overhead flick at the hoops, 1, 3 or 5 points by
  distance. Range rules from cast one: check behind you, rod tip away from
  faces, nobody forward of the line until it's called clear.
- Safety and hygiene calls: fire scenarios - crumbling bank, wet rocks,
  fishing from a boat. Right call scores: back from the edge, lifejacket on
  before you board, sit low, tell someone your plan; plaster any cut, never
  put line or bait in your mouth, wipe hands before food.
- Habitat match: pair fish cards to water cards - pike in weedy stillwater,
  brown trout in fast stony rivers, carp in warm lakes, flounder on estuary
  mud - then defend one match out loud: food, oxygen, cover.
- Legal round, true or false: rod licence from 13 (free for juniors),
  permission or a day ticket for the water, rivers close in spring; gates
  left as found, every scrap of line goes home, quiet on the bank.
- Check-clean-dry relay: the kit "arrives from another water" - check boots
  and nets for hitchhikers, scrub in the bowl, towel dry, and name one
  invasive (killer shrimp, floating pennywort) to seal the win.
