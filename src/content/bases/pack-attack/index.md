A mountain of kit in the middle of the floor, and some of it is sabotage -
the hairdryer, the glass bottle, the cast-iron pan. Two teams, two
rucksacks, and the expedition leaves at the whistle.

- Sort before you stuff: pick what the journey actually needs - sleeping bag
  and mat, dry layers, waterproofs, first aid kit, torch, food, your share
  of tent and stove - and abandon the decoys. Every decoy packed costs
  points; every essential left behind costs more.
- Pack it right: liner first, sleeping bag in the bottom, heavy kit low and
  tight against your back, waterproofs and first aid reachable without
  unpacking, the hourly stuff in the lid.
- Scrutineering: the leader unpacks both bags in front of everyone, scoring
  every call, then the packer shoulders the bag for a lap of the hall - a
  load that swings or pulls backwards gives points away.
- Stove and lamp pit: hands on the unlit stove and lamp. Canisters changed
  outside, away from any flame, seals checked; both live on flat ground in
  the kitchen area and never inside a tent - carbon monoxide has no smell.
  Scenario cards rapid-fire for bonus points.

If you wouldn't carry it for two hours, it doesn't get packed.
