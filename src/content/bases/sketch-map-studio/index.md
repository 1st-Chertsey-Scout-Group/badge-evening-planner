Two example maps go up: one a stranger could follow, one useless memory
scrawl. Spot the difference, then draw the first kind - twice.

- Autopsy the bad map: no north arrow, no road names, turns missing,
  nothing at the junctions. The good one has a landmark at every turn,
  names, rough distances - right order matters more than right scale.
- Warm-up, five minutes: sketch the meeting place itself - building, gate,
  car park, which way the road runs and where it goes.
- The commission: draw a destination card each and map the route from HQ -
  to the chip shop, the park, the church. A stranger must be able to walk
  it: every turn earns a landmark, every road its name.
- The rural commission: from the OS extract, redraw farm-to-village as a
  sketch map. Different furniture out here - stiles, barns, the stream
  crossing, field edges - same rule: a stranger travels it or it fails.
- Field test, where the points fall: swap with another patrol, who walk it
  aloud, ruthlessly literal - leader plays the stranger ("you said second
  left; this is a driveway") - checked against street map or OS extract.
  Every missing turn, nameless road or absent landmark drops a point.

Fewest faults wins the wall; a map you have to explain out loud has failed.
