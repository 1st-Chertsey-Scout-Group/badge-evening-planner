Nobody knows which wall is north yet. Hand over a compass - the red end of
the needle always finds it - and turn the hall into a giant compass rose.

- Tape N to the wall the needle picks, then the patrol lays the other three
  cardinal points: Never Eat Shredded Wheat, clockwise from N.
- Compass twister: leader calls points, scouts sprint to touch the right
  card, last one there judges the next round. Add the in-between four - NE,
  SE, SW, NW - and speed up until it falls apart.
- Champions' decider: blindfold, three spins, "point south-west". The rose
  in their head is the one they keep.
- Set a map with the compass: lay it flat and turn map and all until its
  grid lines run with the needle, north edge to the red end. Now the map
  agrees with the world: printed roads run the way real ones do.
- Set it without: cover the needle and use what you know. Point through the
  walls at the main road and the church, and turn the map until its symbols
  line up with the real things.
- Thumbing lap: map set, thumb pinned on HQ, walk a slow lap of the hall.
  The scout turns at each corner; the map never does. Spot-check anyone
  mid-lap - "point north" - and hesitation sends them to the back.
