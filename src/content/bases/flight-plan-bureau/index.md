The bureau has a client: a pilot flying between two towns at least 100
nautical miles apart, and the patrol builds the plan. Stamp every stage to
score it.

- Draw the track: choose the two places, rule the line between them, measure
  the exact distance against the chart scale. Within two miles of the
  leader's master answer earns the first stamp.
- Fly a pencil along the route and list what the pilot sees in the first half
  hour of clear weather: coastline, motorways, rivers, railways, lakes,
  towns, masts. Ten landmarks in order, second stamp.
- Time the flight: distance over airspeed, times sixty - at 120 knots a
  100-mile track takes 50 minutes. Then the wind cards land: a 20-knot
  headwind, a 30-knot tailwind - rework the time with each new groundspeed.
- Drift round: three heading-and-track pairs per patrol; for each, draw the
  two arrows from one point, measure the angle between them, and label the
  drift in degrees, port or starboard.

Final stamp for briefing the plan back in under a minute - distance,
landmarks, still-air time, worst-wind time.
