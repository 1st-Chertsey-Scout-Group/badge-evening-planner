---
title: Speed portrait studio
description: Three timed drawing rounds - story scene, live model, landscape - then a poster commission that sells Scouts to a kid who has never been.
duration: 20
equipment:
  - A4 cartridge paper, four sheets per scout
  - Charcoal sticks and soft 4B pencils (paints only if cleanup allows)
  - Story extract cards, vivid two-minute reads
  - Model props bag (rucksack, neckerchief, lantern, hat)
  - Masking tape for the gallery wall and sticker dots for voting
tags:
  - Scouts
  - Creative
  - Indoor
requirements:
  - '49088'
  - '75633'
---

Chairs become easels, charcoal out: the studio takes commissions against
the clock, four minutes a round, down tools on the buzzer.

- Round one, the story scene: read the extract card aloud twice - a storm
  swallowing a ship, a dragon circling a city - and they draw the picture
  it made in their head. No two will match; that is the point.
- Round two, the life class: a volunteer poses dead centre in neckerchief
  and rucksack, lantern held high, and holds it. Draw the person - or zoom
  in and draw the object they are gripping. Charcoal forgives shaky hands.
- Round three, the landscape: out the window if there is a view, from
  memory if not - last camp's skyline, tents against trees, counts.
- The commission: one image and five words maximum that would make a kid
  who has never seen Scouts beg to come, drawn as the poster for the next
  camp or the group's open evening. Sell the adventure, not the logo.
- Gallery walk: everything taped to the wall, two sticker dots per scout -
  one for best of show, one for the poster that would actually work. The
  winning poster gets photocopied straight onto the noticeboard.

Charcoal hands wash before anyone touches the biscuit tin.
