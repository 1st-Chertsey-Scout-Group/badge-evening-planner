The only first aid base where doing less scores more. The casualty came off
the climbing wall and landed badly - and the instinct to roll them over is
the thing being trained out.

- Suspect a spine when the story says so: a fall from height, a bike or car
  crash, any blow to the back or neck, or a casualty who can't feel their
  feet. Mechanism first, casualty second.
- The hold: kneel above the head, hands either side, keep head, neck and back
  in one line, and don't let go until help takes over. 999 from a bystander.
- The wiggle test: while one scout holds, a leader plays the helpful adult
  trying to talk them into moving the casualty - "let's get him a chair!".
  Holding firm against bad advice is the win.
- Head injury round: bumped casualties act their card. Mild - sit them down,
  cold compress, watch them. Red flags - drowsy, vomiting, confused, blurred
  or unequal pupils (check with the pen torch on each other) - hospital, now.

Rotate the hold so everyone's hands learn the position. One line to take
home: a damaged spine moved badly can never be unmoved.
